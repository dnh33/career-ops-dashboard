# Danish Tech Salary Benchmarks — Q3 2026

> **Last updated:** 2026-06-30
> **Sources:** Jobindex, Levels.fyi, Glassdoor Denmark, PROSA, IDA (Dansk Ingeniørforening), SalaryExpert, Jobicy, LønRadar, Ansøgningshjælpen, Statistics Denmark (DST), Nucamp
> **Currency:** DKK (Danish Krone) gross monthly unless noted. 1 DKK ≈ 0.13 EUR / 0.14 USD (mid-2026).
> **Methodology:** Figures are compiled from published salary surveys, aggregator platforms, and reported compensation from Danish employers. Where sources conflict, a range is given with the median anchored to the most-referenced source (typically Jobindex/PROSA for general roles, Levels.fyi for large international employers).

---

## 1. How to read this document

| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **P25** | 25th percentile — what the bottom quarter earns (juniors, low-cost locations, smaller companies) |
| **Median** | Typical mid-market offer for a professional with 3–5 years of relevant experience |
| **P75** | 75th percentile — senior specialists, strong negotiators, in-demand stacks |
| **Top 10%** | Staff/principal engineers, leads at top-tier companies (Microsoft, Google, MAANG), or professionals with rare specializations + equity |
| **Total Comp** | Base salary + employer pension contribution + typical bonus (where applicable) |

**Key context for Denmark:**
- Employer pension contribution: **8–14%** of gross salary (tech sector norm: 10–12%)
- Annual vacation: **25 days** statutory minimum (many tech companies offer 28–30)
- Flex hours: standard in tech (core hours 10:00–15:00, rest flexible)
- Remote work: increasingly common; many companies offer 2–3 days WFH/week
- Income tax: progressive; effective rate ~35–42% on gross salary (municipal + state)
- All figures are **pre-tax, gross monthly** unless otherwise noted

## 9. DATA SOURCES & METHODOLOGY NOTES

| Source | Coverage | Date | Reliability |
|--------|----------|------|-------------|
| Jobindex.dk (Løntjek) | All roles, Denmark-wide | 2026 Q2 | High — largest DK job platform |
| LønRadar.dk | Softwareudvikler by city | 2026-05 | High — aggregates Jobindex data |
| Glassdoor Denmark | All roles, self-reported | 2026-06 | Medium — small sample sizes for AI roles |
| Levels.fyi | Large international employers | 2026 Q2 | High for big tech, biased upward |
| PROSA (IT union) | IT professionals, members | 2026 | High — union salary stats |
| IDA | Engineers (civilingeniør) | 2026 | High — comprehensive but engineer-focused |
| SalaryExpert/ERI | Aggregated estimates | 2026 | Medium — modeled, not survey-based |
| Nucamp | AI/ML roles, Denmark | 2026-04 | Medium — bootcamp-affiliated |
| Statistics Denmark (DST/LONS20) | All occupations, official | 2026-06 | Very high but lagged, less granular |
| Ansøgningshjælpen | Softwareudvikler guide | 2026 | Medium — aggregator |

**Limitations:**
- AI/ML roles have smaller sample sizes on Glassdoor/Levels.fyi for Denmark specifically
- Top 10% figures are estimates (few data points at the extreme)
- Remote roles are a growing but still small fraction of the market
- Stock/equity compensation varies wildly and is excluded from base figures
- The "AI Software Developer" vs "AI Engineer" distinction is blurry in practice; many companies use the titles interchangeably

## 10. KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Copenhagen pays 10–15% above national average** for all roles, but cost of living offsets much of this.
2. **Generative AI Engineer is the highest-paid IC role** in Denmark as of Q3 2026, with median 65K/mo in Copenhagen.
3. **AI roles command 15–35% premium over frontend** at mid-level, reflecting acute talent shortage.
4. **Aarhus is a strong secondary market** with 5–10% lower salaries but significantly lower housing costs.
5. **Remote work is narrowing the gap** between cities — candidates in Aalborg/Odense can now access Copenhagen salaries.
6. **Total compensation is 15–25% above base** when pension (10–12%) and benefits are included.
7. **The market is still rising** — IDA reports 4.3% wage growth in 2026 for private sector, with AI specialties growing faster.
